EU and India close in on long-stalled trade pact ahead of June summit

Synthesized from 9 sources · Updated 14m ago

Negotiators from the European Union and India described the latest round of trade talks in Brussels as the most substantive in two years, according to Reuters, which cited two officials briefed on the discussions. The two sides have been working toward a free trade agreement since 2007, with momentum repeatedly stalling over disputes on automotive tariffs and agricultural market access.

The Financial Times reports that schedules on automobiles and wine remain the principal sticking points, though both delegations now expect a framework announcement at the EU–India summit scheduled for late June in New Delhi. A senior Indian commerce ministry official, quoted by The Hindu, said the gap on automotive tariffs had narrowed but stopped short of confirming a final figure.

If concluded, the deal would be the largest trade agreement either side has signed in a decade, covering goods, services, and investment protections. Bloomberg notes that India is the EU's tenth-largest trading partner, with bilateral trade in goods reaching €124 billion in the last reported year. European negotiators have linked progress to Indian commitments on labour standards and intellectual property enforcement, which earlier rounds had described as non-negotiable.

Civil society groups in both jurisdictions have raised concerns about the lack of public consultation on chapters touching on data protection and pharmaceutical patents, though neither government has published a draft text.

What's next The EU–India summit on 24–25 June in New Delhi is expected to either announce a framework agreement or signal a further extension of talks into the autumn.
